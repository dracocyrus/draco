Information about Health Implications of E-Waste


Electronic waste, or e-waste, consists of discarded electronic devices such as computers, phones, and appliances. Toxic chemicals in e-waste can leach into the environment and harm human health. These include substances like lead, mercury, cadmium, and flame retardants, which have been linked to serious health issues such as cancer, neurological damage, and reproductive issues.

E-waste is becoming one of the fastest-growing waste streams in the world, and improper recycling methods exacerbate the issue by exposing people to harmful substances. This is particularly problematic in developing countries where informal recycling processes are common.


Exposure to these substances can cause a wide range of health problems, including:


Respiratory issues: Difficulty breathing, asthma, lung cancer

Neurological disorders: Damage to the nervous system, learning disabilities, impaired cognitive function

Reproductive problems: Birth defects, infertility, miscarriage

Developmental issues: Impaired growth and development in children

Cancer: Increased risk of various types of cancer

Kidney damage: Impaired kidney function

Immune system suppression: Weakened immune response

Vulnerable Populations


Certain groups are particularly vulnerable to the effects of e-waste:


Children: Developing bodies are more susceptible to the harmful effects of toxins.

Pregnant women: Exposure can harm the developing fetus.

Workers in the informal e-waste recycling sector: These individuals are often exposed to high levels of toxic substances.
